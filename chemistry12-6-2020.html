No.1 Which reaction is a neutralisation reaction?

  1. [AgNO3 + HCL= AgCl+HNO3]
  2. [CaCO3= CaO + CO2]
  3. [4Na+O2 = 2Na2O]
  4. [2NaOH + H2SO4 = Na2SO4 + 2H2O]

So, we need to highlight *Neutralisation*. Neutralisation is the process of neutralising an acid and a base with results therefore being salt and water

In short words, Acid + Base = Salt+ Water with acid = [H plus] and base being = [OH minus]

And the only eligible answer is No.4 with Base being 2NaOH, H2SO4 being acid, Na2SO4 being salt, and 2H2O being water.

No.2 Elements W and X are metals. Elements Y and Z are non-metals. The oxides of W,X,Y, and Z all form solutions when added to water. Which statement is correct?

  1. [The solution of the oxide of element W turns blue litmus red.]
  2. [The solution of the oxide of the element X fizzes when sodium carbonate is added]
  3. [The solution of the oxide of element Y has a pH greaten than pH 7]
  4. [The solution of the oxide of element Z fizzes when magnesium is added]

As we know, metals= W and X= Base while non-metals=Y and Z= Acids. So the only correct statement is No.4
